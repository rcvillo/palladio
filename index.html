ANDREA PALLADIO

Andrea Palladio was a renowned architect from 16th century Northern Italy. His vast knowledge of ancient architecture allowed for an “inventive interpretation of classical tradition,” such as a strict system of structure and decoration, use of ancient Roman columns, entablatures, pediments, and his application of symmetry and balance. This vast knowledge resulted not only in numerous commissions for palaces, villas, and churches throughout Northern Italy, but also in a written guide to the antiquity of Rome, and the publication of four books of architecture. Consequently, these written works served as a guideline for architecture in America, making him “the most imitated architect of all time.”
